Code Of Conduct

ADVISORY

Dear ,As we embark on a new academic session, we must work collaboratively to foster a productive, well-disciplined, and inspiring learning environment.

Each of us plays a vital role in shaping a positive schoolculture that supports academic excellence, personal growth, and holistic development.

Please adhere to the following guidelines to ensure the smooth and efficient functioning of our school.Your dedication, professionalism, and commitment will contribute significantly to creating an enrichingand nurturing atmosphere for our students. Let us work together with a shared vision to make this academicyear successful and rewarding for all.

1. Punctuality & Attendance:- The assembly starts at 8 a.m. Teachers are expected to be in the assembly area with the classes inproper line formation by then.

Kindly arrive on time and fulfill all assigned duties.Notify the administration in advance in case of leave or absence, or any hurdle in coming to schooltimely.

Any short leave should not exceed 30 minutes. Please adhere to this limit and avoid situations thatmay lead to its denial or take leave.

Do not approach the undersigned for exceptions or leave requests.

2. Professional Conduct:-Maintain a respectful and positive attitude towards students, colleagues, and parents by fostering aculture of mutual respect, empathy, and open communication.

Encourage a supportive and inclusiveenvironment where everyone feels valued and heard.

Handle conflicts professionally, listen actively, andaddress concerns with patience and understanding. Uphold professional decorum in all interactions,ensuring a collaborative and harmonious school atmosphere.

Foster an atmosphere of dignity and comfort,avoiding language or gestures that may be perceived as harsh or inappropriate.

All are expected tofamiliarize themselves with and follow the POSH guidelines.

Always uphold the highest ethical and professional standards in your conduct, decisions, andinteractions, ensuring integrity, fairness, and respect.

Avoid any form of groupism or bias, and treateveryone with impartiality and professionalism. Foster a culture of collaboration and teamwork,supporting colleagues and working together to create a positive and productive learning environment

.3. Classroom Management & Student Discipline:-Create a dynamic and well-structured learning environment that fosters curiosity, active participation, anddiscipline among students. Encourage interactive and student-centered learning through innovativeteaching methods that make lessons engaging and meaningful.

Establish clear expectations for behavior,ensuring a respectful and orderly atmosphere where students feel safe, valued, and motivated to learn.

Address disciplinary matters with fairness and consistency, reinforcing positive behavior andinstilling a sense of responsibility and self-discipline in students.

Maintain a comprehensive profileregister for each student, recording significant academic and behavioral instances, both positive andnegative.

This register should track progress, achievements, challenges, and any notable incidents toensure a holistic view of each student’s development.

 Use of foul language, disrespect, or misconduct will result in strict disciplinary action

. Regular follow-ups on student discipline and academic performance are mandatory.

 No teacher is permitted to send any student out of the classroom for any task during the period, exceptin genuine emergencies.

The term emergency must be interpreted strictly in its true sense.Show the students’ profile register to the undersigned fortnightly.

If no records of earlier misdeeds andcorresponding actions taken are found, the teacher will be held responsible for the lack of documentation,follow-up, and corrective counselling.

Failure to document and address issues appropriately may imply thatthe teacher has inadvertently encouraged the behavior by neglecting it.

4. Lesson Planning &amp; Teaching - Plan lessons strategically in advance to ensure structured andeffective learning. Implement innovative and student-centered teaching strategies.

5. Assessment of teaching and learning Weekly tests must be conducted for all students without exception.

Teachers are required to assessstudents on all criteria through a thorough, well-documented evaluation, not a mere formality.

Centralized monthly tests will be conducted at the school level.

Teachers must ensure that the questionpaper is of an appropriate standard and rigor, maintaining the required academic level.

6. School Cleanliness Safety- Maintain cleanliness and hygiene in classrooms and commonareas. Adhere to all safety protocols to protect students and staff

7. Extra-Curricular EngagementPromote active student participation in sports, arts, and cultural programs to ensure holisticdevelopment beyond academics.

Encourage students to explore their talents, build teamwork, and developconfidence through various extracurricular activities.

Actively contribute to school events and foster alively and inclusive school culture.

Support and participate in organizing academic, cultural, and co-curricular activities to create a vibrant learning environment.

8. Technology Record-Keeping - Leverage digital tools for enhanced teaching and efficientrecord management.

Ensure accurate and systematic record-keeping of student attendance,performance, and progress and profile.

9. Confidentiality &amp; Integrity -Ensure the confidentiality and security of student and school-related information.

By strictly adhering to these guidelines, we can ensure a disciplined, structured, and enrichingeducational environment for our students. Let us work together with dedication and accountability tomake this academic year a resounding success.

Note: Until the skill teacher joins, foundational subject teachers must take initiative in leading skillclasses with structure and purpose. These sessions should be engaging, meaningful, and aligned withthe teachers&#39; expertise, skills, or talents. Creativity and innovation are essential to making learningimpactful.

Idle time is not acceptable—every moment should contribute to students growth anddevelopment.
